Professional Pressure Where It Belongs

Pressure washing is best used on hard surfaces that can handle controlled force. It should not be treated as the default for siding, painted surfaces, or delicate materials.

Surfaces cleaned

  • Concrete driveways
  • Sidewalks and walkways
  • Patios and pool surrounds
  • Curbs and exterior hardscape
  • Commercial entrances

When to use it

  • Heavy dirt and surface staining
  • High-traffic exterior areas
  • Pre-listing curb appeal
  • Seasonal property maintenance
  • Storefront and entry presentation

Is pressure washing right for every surface?
No. Pressure washing is best for durable hard surfaces. Soft washing is usually safer for siding, painted finishes, and more delicate exterior surfaces.
Can pressure washing improve curb appeal?
Yes. Cleaning stained concrete, walkways, patios, and high-traffic areas can make a property look brighter and better maintained.
